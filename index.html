Tariff Impact by Country

Understanding the Impact

This bar chart shows the projected trade deficit impact of US tariffs on different countries. Countries marked in red have limited digital infrastructure access, making them more vulnerable to tariff changes. The height of each bar represents the trade deficit in millions of USD, showing which countries are most affected by the new tariff policies.

Trade Deficit vs. Digital Infrastructure Access

Digital Divide and Trade Impact

This scatter plot reveals the correlation between digital infrastructure access and trade deficits. Each point represents a country, with the size indicating population. The position shows the relationship between digital access (x-axis) and trade deficit (y-axis). Countries in the lower right quadrant have better digital infrastructure and lower trade deficits, while those in the upper left face both digital limitations and higher trade deficits.

Tariff Impact on Employment

Employment Consequences

This visualization estimates the potential job losses in export-dependent industries due to the new tariffs. The chart shows the percentage of jobs at risk in each country's export sector, with special emphasis on countries with limited digital infrastructure. The data suggests that countries with poor digital access may face more severe employment impacts due to their limited ability to adapt to changing trade conditions.
